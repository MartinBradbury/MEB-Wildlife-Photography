The Peak District

The Peak District is at the Southern Area of the Pennines. It was the first of the National Parks of England and Wales in 1951. The highest point in the Peak District is Kinder Scout at 2,087 ft (636m). The National Park covers 555 square miles and it includes the Castleton caves, Mam Tor, Winnats Pass and Dovedale.
